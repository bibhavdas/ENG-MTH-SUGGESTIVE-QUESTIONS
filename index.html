Part 1: Vector Calculus & Partial Differentiation (Q1 - Q8)
1. What is the condition for two vectors \(\vec{a}\) & \(\vec{b}\) to be perpendicular and to be parallel?

Solution:

  • Perpendicular (Orthogonal): Two non-zero vectors are perpendicular if their scalar (dot) product is zero. $$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = 0 $$
  • Parallel: Two non-zero vectors are parallel if their vector (cross) product is the zero vector. $$ \vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \vec{0} $$
2. Find the angle between the vectors \(\vec{a} = \langle 2, 2, -1 \rangle\) & \(\vec{b} = \langle 5, -3, 2 \rangle\). Find \(\vec{a} \times \vec{b}\) and a vector that is orthogonal to both \(\vec{a}\) & \(\vec{b}\).

Solution:

1. Angle (\(\theta\)):
Using \(\cos \theta = \frac{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}}{|\vec{a}| |\vec{b}|}\):
\(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = (2)(5) + (2)(-3) + (-1)(2) = 10 - 6 - 2 = 2\)
\(|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{2^2 + 2^2 + (-1)^2} = \sqrt{9} = 3\)
\(|\vec{b}| = \sqrt{5^2 + (-3)^2 + 2^2} = \sqrt{38}\)
Thus, \(\theta = \cos^{-1}\left( \frac{2}{3\sqrt{38}} \right)\).

2. Cross Product (\(\vec{a} \times \vec{b}\)):
$$ \vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ 2 & 2 & -1 \\ 5 & -3 & 2 \end{vmatrix} = \langle 1, -9, -16 \rangle $$

3. Orthogonal Vector:
By definition, the cross product \(\langle 1, -9, -16 \rangle\) is orthogonal to both \(\vec{a}\) and \(\vec{b}\).

3. When are 3 vectors \(\vec{a}, \vec{b}, \vec{c}\) coplanar? Are the vectors \(\vec{a}=\langle 1,1,2 \rangle, \vec{b}=\langle 1,-2,1 \rangle\) & \(\vec{c}=\langle 3,-1,5 \rangle\) coplanar?

Condition: Three vectors are coplanar if their Scalar Triple Product is zero: \([\vec{a}\ \vec{b}\ \vec{c}] = 0\).

Verification:
$$ \det \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 & 2 \\ 1 & -2 & 1 \\ 3 & -1 & 5 \end{pmatrix} $$ $$ = 1(-10 - (-1)) - 1(5 - 3) + 2(-1 - (-6)) $$ $$ = 1(-9) - 2 + 2(5) = -9 - 2 + 10 = -1 $$

Since \(-1 \neq 0\), the vectors are not coplanar.

4. Find a vector that is perpendicular to the plane that passes through the points \(P(1,4,6)\), \(Q(-2,5,-1)\) & \(R(1,-1,1)\).

Solution:

Find two vectors lying on the plane:

  • \(\vec{PQ} = Q - P = \langle -3, 1, -7 \rangle\)
  • \(\vec{PR} = R - P = \langle 0, -5, -5 \rangle\)

The normal vector \(\vec{n}\) is the cross product \(\vec{PQ} \times \vec{PR}\):
$$ \vec{n} = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ -3 & 1 & -7 \\ 0 & -5 & -5 \end{vmatrix} = \langle -40, -15, 15 \rangle $$

Dividing by -5, a simpler perpendicular vector is \(\langle 8, 3, -3 \rangle\).

5. If \(f(x,y,z) = x^{y/z}\), then find all the 1st order & 2nd order partial derivatives of \(f\).

Solution:

Rewrite function: \(f = e^{\ln(x^{y/z})} = e^{\frac{y}{z}\ln x}\).

1st Order Derivatives:

  • \(f_x = \frac{y}{z} x^{(\frac{y}{z}-1)}\)
  • \(f_y = x^{y/z} \ln(x) \cdot \frac{1}{z}\)
  • \(f_z = x^{y/z} \ln(x) \cdot \left(-\frac{y}{z^2}\right)\)

2nd Order Derivatives (Examples):

  • \(f_{xx} = \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \left( \frac{y}{z} x^{\frac{y}{z}-1} \right) = \frac{y}{z} \left(\frac{y}{z}-1\right) x^{\frac{y}{z}-2}\)
  • \(f_{yy}\), \(f_{zz}\), and mixed derivatives are found similarly using product/chain rules.
6. Find \(\frac{\partial z}{\partial x}\) & \(\frac{\partial z}{\partial y}\) when:
(i) \(x^2 + 2y^2 + 3z^2 = 1\)
(ii) \(yz + x \log y = z^2\)

Solution: Use Implicit Differentiation formula: \(\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = -\frac{F_x}{F_z}\), \(\frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = -\frac{F_y}{F_z}\).

(i) \(F = x^2 + 2y^2 + 3z^2 - 1 = 0\)
\(\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = -\frac{2x}{6z} = -\frac{x}{3z}\)
\(\frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = -\frac{4y}{6z} = -\frac{2y}{3z}\)

(ii) \(F = yz + x \ln y - z^2 = 0\)
\(F_x = \ln y\), \(F_y = z + \frac{x}{y}\), \(F_z = y - 2z\).
\(\frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = -\frac{\ln y}{y - 2z}\)
\(\frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = -\frac{z + \frac{x}{y}}{y - 2z}\)

7. Find \(\frac{\partial z}{\partial s}\) & \(\frac{\partial z}{\partial t}\) when:
(i) \(z = x^2 y^3\), \(x = s \cos t\), \(y = s \sin t\)
(ii) \(z = \tan(u/v)\), \(u = 2s^2 + 3t\), \(v = \sqrt{s^2+t^3}\)

Solution: Apply the Multivariable Chain Rule.

(i):
\(\frac{\partial z}{\partial s} = \frac{\partial z}{\partial x}\frac{\partial x}{\partial s} + \frac{\partial z}{\partial y}\frac{\partial y}{\partial s}\)
\(= (2xy^3)(\cos t) + (3x^2y^2)(\sin t)\)

(ii):
\(\frac{\partial z}{\partial s} = \frac{\partial z}{\partial u}\frac{\partial u}{\partial s} + \frac{\partial z}{\partial v}\frac{\partial v}{\partial s}\)
Calculate components: \(\frac{\partial z}{\partial u} = \frac{1}{v}\sec^2(u/v)\), \(\frac{\partial u}{\partial s} = 4s\), etc., and substitute.

8. For each (a, b, c): Find (i) \(\nabla f\), (ii) \(D_{\hat{u}}f(P)\).
(a) \(f(x,y) = \sin(2x+3y)\), \(P(-6, 4)\), \(\hat{u} = \langle \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}, -\frac{1}{2} \rangle\)
(b) \(f(x,y) = y^2/x\), \(P(1,2)\), \(\hat{u} = \langle \frac{2}{3}, \frac{\sqrt{5}}{3} \rangle\)
(c) \(f(x,y,z) = y^2 e^{xyz}\), \(P(0, 1, -1)\), \(\hat{u} = \langle \frac{3}{13}, \frac{4}{13}, \frac{12}{13} \rangle\)

(a):
\(\nabla f = \langle 2\cos(2x+3y), 3\cos(2x+3y) \rangle\). At P, arg is 0. \(\nabla f(P) = \langle 2, 3 \rangle\).
\(D_{\hat{u}}f = \langle 2, 3 \rangle \cdot \hat{u} = \sqrt{3} - 1.5\).

(b):
\(\nabla f = \langle -y^2/x^2, 2y/x \rangle\). At P(1,2), \(\nabla f = \langle -4, 4 \rangle\).
\(D_{\hat{u}}f = \frac{1}{3}(-8 + 4\sqrt{5})\).

(c):
At P, \(\nabla f = \langle -1, 2, 0 \rangle\).
\(D_{\hat{u}}f = (-1)(\frac{3}{13}) + (2)(\frac{4}{13}) + 0 = \frac{5}{13}\).

Part 2: Max/Min, Integration & Vector Fields (Q9 - Q13)
9. Find the points of local max, local min & saddle pts for:
(a) \(f(x,y) = xy + \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y}\)
(b) \(f(x,y) = e^y(y^2 - x^2)\)

(a):
\(f_x = y - 1/x^2 = 0\), \(f_y = x - 1/y^2 = 0\).
Solving yields critical point \((1,1)\).
Hessian: \(A=f_{xx}=2, B=f_{xy}=1, C=f_{yy}=2\).
\(AC-B^2 = 3 > 0\) and \(A > 0\). Point (1,1) is a Local Minimum.

(b):
Solve system \(f_x = -2xe^y = 0\) and \(f_y = e^y(y^2 + 2y - x^2) = 0\).
Points: \((0,0)\) and \((0,-2)\). Use Hessian determinant test to classify.

10. Prove that \(f_1\) & \(f_2\) are both homogeneous & comment on their degrees.
\(f_1(x,y) = x^3 - 2x^2y + 3xy^2 + y^3\)
\(f_2(x,y) = x \log(y/x)\)

Solution:

  • \(f_1(tx, ty) = t^3 x^3 - 2t^3 x^2y + 3t^3 xy^2 + t^3 y^3 = t^3 f_1(x,y)\).
    Homogeneous of degree 3.
  • \(f_2(tx, ty) = (tx) \log(ty/tx) = t [x \log(y/x)] = t^1 f_2(x,y)\).
    Homogeneous of degree 1.
11. Evaluate the following double integrals:
(i) \(\int_0^4 \int_0^{\sqrt{y}} x y^2 \, dx \, dy\)
(ii) \(\iint_D \frac{y}{x^5+1} \, dA\), where \(D = \{(x,y) \in \mathbb{R}^2 | 0 \le x \le 1, 0 \le y \le x^2\}\)
(iii) \(\iint_D x^3 \, dA\), where \(D = \{1 \le x \le e, 0 \le y \le \ln x\}\)

(i) Solution:
Inner integral: \([\frac{x^2}{2}]_0^{\sqrt{y}} = \frac{y}{2}\).
Outer integral: \(\int_0^4 \frac{y}{2} \cdot y^2 \, dy = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^4 y^3 \, dy = \frac{1}{2}[\frac{y^4}{4}]_0^4 = 32\).

(ii) Solution:
\(\int_0^1 \int_0^{x^2} \frac{y}{x^5+1} \, dy \, dx\).
Inner: \(\frac{1}{x^5+1} [\frac{y^2}{2}]_0^{x^2} = \frac{x^4}{2(x^5+1)}\).
Outer: Let \(u=x^5+1\). Result: \(\frac{1}{10} \ln 2\).

(iii) Solution:
\(\int_1^e x^3 (\ln x) \, dx\). Use integration by parts. Result: \(\frac{3e^4+1}{16}\).

12. Show that any vector field of the form \(\vec{F} = \langle f(x), g(y), h(z) \rangle\) is conservative & any vector field of the form \(\vec{F} = \langle f(y,z), g(x,z), h(x,y) \rangle\) is solenoidal.

Solution:

  • Conservative: \(\nabla \times \vec{F} = \vec{0}\). Since components depend only on their own variable (e.g., \(P\) only on \(x\)), mixed partials are 0.
  • Solenoidal: \(\nabla \cdot \vec{F} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial g}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial h}{\partial z}\). Since \(f\) doesn't depend on \(x\), etc., divergence is 0.
13. Find \(\text{curl}(\vec{F})\) & \(\text{div}(\vec{F})\) for each vector field \(\vec{F}\):
(a) \(\vec{F} = \langle x+yz, y+xz, z+xy \rangle\)
(b) \(\vec{F} = \langle \frac{x}{D}, \frac{y}{D}, \frac{z}{D} \rangle\), where \(D = \sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}\).

(a):
Div: \(1 + 1 + 1 = 3\).
Curl: \(\vec{0}\) (Conservative field).

(b):
Div: \(\frac{2}{D}\) (using spherical identities).
Curl: \(\vec{0}\).

18. When do we say that a system of linear equations is consistent? Inconsistent? Write conditions.
  • Consistent: If Rank(A) = Rank(Augmented Matrix [A|B]). It has a solution.
  • Inconsistent: If Rank(A) < Rank([A|B]). It has no solution.

20. Investigate the values of \(\lambda\) & \(\mu\) such that the system has (i) no solution, (ii) unique solution, (iii) infinite solutions.
System: \(2x+3y+5z=9\), \(7x+3y-2z=8\), \(2x+3y+\lambda z=\mu\)

Comparing Eq 1 and Eq 3: Subtraction gives \((\lambda - 5)z = \mu - 9\).

  • (i) No Solution: \(\lambda = 5\) and \(\mu \neq 9\).
  • (ii) Unique Solution: \(\lambda \neq 5\) (Determinant is non-zero).
  • (iii) Infinite Solutions: \(\lambda = 5\) and \(\mu = 9\).
21. Verify Cayley-Hamilton Theorem, Find Eigenvalues/Vectors & Diagonalize for given matrices.

Method:

  1. Find Characteristic Equation: \(|A - \lambda I| = 0\).
  2. Cayley-Hamilton: Verify \(A\) satisfies this equation ($A^3 - \dots = 0$).
  3. Find roots (\(\lambda\)) for Eigenvalues.
  4. Find Eigenvectors by solving \((A-\lambda I)X = 0\).
  5. Diagonalizable if number of linearly independent eigenvectors equals dimension of matrix.
22. Define Beta & Gamma functions. Find \(\Gamma(6)\) & \(B(5,4)\). Show \(B(m+1, n) = \frac{m}{m+n}B(m,n)\).

Definitions:
\(\Gamma(n) = \int_0^\infty e^{-x} x^{n-1} dx\)
\(B(m,n) = \int_0^1 x^{m-1} (1-x)^{n-1} dx\)

Values:
\(\Gamma(6) = 5! = 120\).
\(B(5,4) = \frac{\Gamma(5)\Gamma(4)}{\Gamma(9)} = \frac{24 \times 6}{40320} = \frac{1}{280}\).

Proof: Use the relation \(B(m,n) = \frac{\Gamma(m)\Gamma(n)}{\Gamma(m+n)}\) and the property \(\Gamma(x+1) = x\Gamma(x)\).
